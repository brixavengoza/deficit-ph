# Gen Z Taglish Content Style Guide

Write like you're texting your kaibigan - natural, supportive, real talk.

## Core Vibe

- **Tone:** Supportive ate/kuya energy (not corporate, not trying hard)
- **Goal:** Make users feel "gets ko sila" not "sinasabihan ako"
- **Energy:** Positive reinforcement, never guilt-tripping

## Where to Use Taglish

✅ Apply to:

- Screen titles and subtitles
- Onboarding flow copy
- Motivational messages / encouragement
- Empty states ("Wala pang naka-log today...")
- Error messages / helper text
- Success celebrations
- Tooltips and hints
- Push notification copy

❌ Keep in English:

- Button labels ("Log Food", "Save", "Continue")
- Navigation tabs ("Home", "Progress", "Profile")
- Form labels ("Email", "Password")
- Technical terms ("API", "Settings")
- Numbers and units ("1,450 kcal", "65 kg")

## Language Patterns

### Natural Code-Switching Rules

Filipino Gen Z naturally switches based on **word category**:

**Use English for:**

- Tech terms: calories, deficit, macros, log, track, progress
- Action verbs in UI context: save, delete, edit, share
- Fitness terms: protein, carbs, workout, goal
- Modern concepts: mindset, vibe, consistency

**Use Tagalog for:**

- Connectors: kasi, pero, tapos, kung, para
- Feelings: proud, galing, kaya mo, pagod
- Time: ngayon, bukas, kahapon, araw-araw
- Questions: ano, paano, bakit, saan
- Encouragement: laban, tuloy, go, sige

**Mix naturally:**

- "Kumain ka na ba?" not "Have you eaten?"
- "Track mo yung lunch mo" not "I-track ang iyong tanghalian"
- "Galing mo today!" not "You did great today!"

### Sentence Structures That Sound Natural

**Good (Gen Z Taglish):**

- "Nag-log ka na ba today?"
- "Konti na lang, 200 cal pa!"
- "Wow, galing mo this week!"
- "Ready ka na mag-start?"
- "Type lang, bahala na ako!"

**Bad (Trying hard/Formal):**

- "Nag-record ka na ba ngayong araw?"
- "Kaunti na lamang, 200 calories pa!"
- "Napakahusay mo ngayong linggo!"
- "Handa ka na bang magsimula?"
- "I-type mo lamang, ako na ang bahala!"

### Pronouns & Addressing

Use **ikaw/ka/mo** (casual you):

- "Kumain ka na?" ✅
- "Kumain na kayo?" ❌ (too formal)
- "You ate already?" ❌ (too English)

Use **ako/ko** (I/me) when app is speaking:

- "Naiintindihan ko yan!" ✅
- "I understand that!" ❌

Keep it **conversational**, like you're texting:

- "Gets ko na! 2 itlog, rice, kape. Tama?" ✅
- "Naintindihan ko na po. 2 itlog, kanin, kape. Tama po ba?" ❌
